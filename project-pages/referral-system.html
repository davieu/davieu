Referral-System

Collaborative fullstack program developed for non-profit organizations to refer their clients to one another.

Worked as a backend-developer in this full-stack application that included a team of six to simulate an Agile workspace environment. The teams were split into two main groups of either frontend or backend. We had a project-manager whom was one of our classmates. The product owner was one of our instructors who ultimately decided what our project was and how he wanted it completed. We had a seven day stretch to complete what we all thought was a walk in the park.

Our first few days was mainly planning and figuring out the flow of the app and the data-structure. We ended up using a NoSQL database since I think we all agreed that we was more familiar with MongoDB than SQL. Once we got that sorted both departments began working on their sprints backlogs. The first few days went smoothly with people burning down the charts on Trello, even to a point where we ran out of tasks(bad planning). When that happened some arguments and disagreements ensued within the group. Aside from that, the next day we had our scrum meeting and we all discussed major concerns and cleared out the bad air.

On deployment and demo day we had a roadblock with deploying but eventually got it done. This group project I believe was really important for us as a whole since it simulated a real product and it showed us how to work in groups and collabing with GitHub.

For me this overall was an unbelievable experience and demonstrated how to work through adversity and communicate as a group to finish a product.
